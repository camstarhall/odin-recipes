Beef ragu

Description

Indulge in the rich flavors of our classic beef ragu. Tender chunks of beef are slow-cooked in a savory tomato-based sauce, enhanced with aromatic vegetables and Italian herbs. This hearty dish pairs perfectly with al dente pasta, creating a comforting and satisfying meal that will leave you craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds (680g) beef chuck roast, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried Italian herbs (such as oregano, basil, thyme)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound (450g) pasta (such as fettuccine or pappardelle)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Steps:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  2. Season the beef cubes with salt and pepper, then add them to the pot. Cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Saut√© for 5 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  4. Add the minced garlic and tomato paste to the pot and cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
  5. Pour in the red wine (if using) and cook for a few minutes, scraping the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits.
  6. Return the beef to the pot and add the crushed tomatoes, beef broth, dried Italian herbs, and bay leaf. Stir well to combine.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the ragu simmer for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally. For even more flavor, you can simmer it for up to 4 hours.
  8. While the ragu is simmering, cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta and set it aside.
  9. Once the ragu has finished simmering, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Remove the bay leaf.
  10. Serve the ragu over the cooked pasta, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped parsley if desired.
